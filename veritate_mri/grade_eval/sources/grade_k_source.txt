Maya was excited because today was the first day of school. She had a new backpack with red flowers on it. Her mother had packed her a sandwich and an apple for lunch. The sandwich was peanut butter and jelly, her favorite kind.

Maya and her mother walked to the bus stop together. There were three other children waiting. Two of them were brothers, and the third was a girl Maya did not know. The girl had long brown hair tied with a yellow ribbon.

The bus came around the corner with a loud rumbling sound. The driver opened the door and waved at the children. Maya climbed up the steps and looked for a seat. The girl with the yellow ribbon sat down next to her.

"Hi, I'm Lucy," the girl said with a smile. "What's your name?"

"I'm Maya," Maya said. "Is this your first day too?"

"It is," Lucy said. "I'm a little nervous. Are you?"

"Yes, but I think it will be okay."

The bus rolled on. They passed houses and a small park and a store with a yellow sign. Maya watched everything go by through the window. She had taken this bus before, but never alone, and never to a new school.

When the bus stopped, the children got off in a group. The school was a brick building with a wide front door and a flag flying above it. There were big trees on either side of the path that led to the entrance.

Inside, the hallway smelled like fresh paint and something sweet, maybe chalk or paper. A teacher with kind eyes was standing near the door. She had a clipboard and was checking off names as children arrived.

"Maya," Maya said when it was her turn. "And this is Lucy. She is in my class too."

"Welcome, both of you," the teacher said. "Mrs. Diaz is your teacher. Her room is right down the hall, second door on the right."

Maya and Lucy walked together. The classroom had bright posters on the walls and small desks arranged in two neat rows. A woman with curly black hair turned to greet them.

"Good morning, girls," she said. "I'm Mrs. Diaz. Find a desk and put your backpacks underneath."

Maya chose a desk near the window. Lucy sat right next to her. They smiled at each other. Maya was glad she had made a friend already.

As more children came in, the room filled with quiet voices. A boy with glasses sat in front of Maya. Two boys behind her were already whispering about a video game. Mrs. Diaz waited until everyone was settled, then closed the door.

"This year, we will learn many things," she said. "We will read books, write stories, do math, and learn about science. But the most important thing is that we will be a kind class. Does anyone know what kind means?"

The boy with glasses raised his hand. "It means being nice to people."

"Yes, that's right. And it means listening when someone is talking, helping when someone needs help, and being a good friend. Can you all promise to be kind?"

"Yes," the class said together.

The morning passed quickly. They learned each other's names, played a game where they had to find someone with the same favorite color, and started reading a book about a rabbit who lost his way home. Mrs. Diaz read in a soft, steady voice that made Maya feel calm.

When the bell rang for lunch, the children lined up at the door. Maya carried her lunch bag carefully. She did not want her sandwich to get squished.

The cafeteria was big and noisy. Children were sitting at long tables, eating and talking. Maya and Lucy found two empty seats next to each other.

"Your sandwich looks good," Lucy said.

"Thanks. My mom always makes it just the way I like."

Lucy had brought a turkey sandwich with cheese. She also had carrot sticks and a banana. They traded one carrot stick for a piece of apple, and both of them laughed because it felt grown-up.

After lunch, the class went outside for recess. The playground had two slides, four swings, and a big wooden fort with a rope ladder. Maya and Lucy ran straight to the swings. They pumped their legs hard until they were swinging high enough to feel the wind in their hair.

"This is the best day," Maya said.

"Me too," Lucy agreed.

When recess was over, they went back inside for math. Mrs. Diaz had drawn shapes on the board. There were circles, squares, triangles, and rectangles. Each child had to come up and point to a shape and say its name. Maya pointed to a triangle and said the word loud and clear. Mrs. Diaz nodded with a smile.

The afternoon passed even faster than the morning. They drew pictures of their families, listened to another chapter of the rabbit book, and learned a song about days of the week. By the time the bell rang for the end of school, Maya felt happy and a little tired.

On the bus ride home, Lucy waved goodbye when Maya got off at her stop. "See you tomorrow!" she called. "See you tomorrow!" Maya called back.

Maya's mother was waiting at the corner. "How was your first day?" she asked.

"It was wonderful," Maya said. "I made a new friend. Her name is Lucy. We sit next to each other."

"That's wonderful, sweetie. I'm so happy for you."

They walked home together holding hands. The sky was bright and blue. A few clouds floated lazily by. A neighbor was watering her garden, and she waved when they passed. Maya waved back with both hands.

At home, Maya took her sandwich wrapper out of her bag and put it in the trash. She showed her mother the picture she had drawn. Her mother put it on the fridge with a magnet shaped like a strawberry.

"Tomorrow will be even better," her mother said.

Maya nodded. She believed it.
