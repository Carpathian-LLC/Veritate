- candidate angles to review (no retrain, sidecar-style, ride alongside the checkpoint):
    - inference-time slot table: small fp side-memory that pins recent topical / entity bytes and biases logits at decode. catches partial-word cul-de-sacs and topic wander without touching QAT weights.
    - logit-space adapters: tiny fp head trained on a frozen base that re-shapes the output distribution. adapter is the only thing that learns; base stays ternary.
    - constrained / guided decoding: dictionary-trie + repetition penalty + n-gram block. cheap, deterministic, fixes spelling cul-de-sacs and loops at the sampler level.
    - speculative byte-chunking: draft 4-8 bytes ahead, accept only if a coherence check (entropy floor, dictionary hit, slot-table agreement) passes. amortizes coherence over chunks instead of bytes.
    - router temperature / top-k anneal at inference: MEGA already routes top-1 per block; loosening to top-2 with a small mix can break router collapse symptoms without retraining experts.
    - aux expert sidecar: freeze the 8 trained experts, add a 9th tiny fp expert trained on a small high-quality slice, route to it only when uncertainty spikes. extends knowledge without rewriting the base.
    - retrieval / corpus echo: at decode, look up the last N bytes in the training corpus index and softly bias toward its continuations. byte-level RAG, no embedding model needed.

ideas
- multi-mind directions for after the fluency fix lands:
    - more experts, smaller each: 16-32 experts at the same active budget pushes specialization further (spelling vs prose vs code vs numerics vs structure).
    - hierarchical routing: per-block router picks a "domain", a second router picks the expert. lets the model carve coarse regions before fine ones.
    - asymmetric experts: not all experts need the same shape. one wide-shallow expert for spelling, one narrow-deep for reasoning, one tiny one for punctuation/structure.
    - cross-block expert sharing: tie a "spelling" expert across early blocks so orthography circuits don't get re-learned per layer.
    - sidecar mind: a separate small model (not an expert, a peer) that runs alongside and votes on the next byte. cheap consistency check.
